## 2. The "2025 Meta": Reasoning & Generative AI

The technical requirements for a Competition GM have evolved significantly. You are no longer just competing on accuracy; you are competing on **reasoning** and **efficiency**.

- **AIMO (AI Mathematical Olympiad):** These high-stakes competitions in 2025 focus on models that can solve complex, international-level math problems. To win here, GMs are moving away from simple fine-tuning and toward **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting** and **Self-Correction loops**.

- **The Efficiency Constraint:** Many 2025 competitions are "Efficiency Tracks." You might be limited to a specific T4 or P100 GPU quota, forcing you to master **Quantization** (4-bit/8-bit), **LoRA**, and **Flash Attention** to fit large models into small memory footprints.

- **Tabular Renaissance:** For classic data competitions, the "Grandmaster Playbook" now relies on GPU-accelerated stacking. Using tools like `cuML` and `cuDF`, GMs now train hundreds of diverse models (XGBoost, CatBoost, Deep Learning) and stack them in 3 or even 4 levels of hierarchy to squeeze out the final 0.0001% of performance.

## 3. The New "Game Arena" Frontier

Kaggle has introduced the **Game Arena** (e.g., Chess, Poker, and Werewolf simulations), where you achieve Grandmastery by building agents that compete in real-time. This requires a deep understanding of **Reinforcement Learning (RL)** and **Game Theory**, adding a "strategic" path to GM status that didn't exist in the early years of the platform.
