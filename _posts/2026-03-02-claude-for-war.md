## **Military Applications in Operation Epic Fury**

According to reports from the *Wall Street Journal* and *The Guardian*, the U.S. military utilized Claude's advanced reasoning and data-processing capabilities for several critical tasks during the initial bombardment of Iranian facilities:

* **Intelligence Synthesis:** Claude was used to process massive streams of real-time intelligence data, including the structuring of satellite imagery and thermal maps to identify anomalies.
* **Target Selection:** The model assisted in identifying and prioritizing military objects, such as missile launchers and command centers, while modeling potential collateral damage.
* **Battlefield Simulations:** Commanders used the AI to run "digital twin" simulations of the Persian Gulf theater, calculating escalation scenarios and predicting Iranian retaliatory strikes.

## **The "Ban vs. Necessity" Paradox**

The use of Claude during the war created a significant political firestorm due to a direct clash between the White House and the Pentagon:

1. **The Official Ban:** On February 28, 2026, President Trump ordered all federal agencies to "immediately cease" using Anthropic's technology, labeling the company a "Radical Left AI company" and a "supply chain risk."
2. **Military Dependence:** Despite the order, the Pentagon continued using Claude during the Iran strikes. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth noted that while the military would transition to other providers (like OpenAI) over a six-month period, the system was too "intricately embedded" in current operations to be shut down mid-strike without risking American lives.
3. **The Friction:** The conflict stems from Anthropic's refusal to remove safety guardrails. CEO Dario Amodei has consistently blocked the use of Claude for **fully autonomous weapons** (taking humans "out of the loop") and **mass domestic surveillance**, which led to the administration's "supply chain risk" designation.
